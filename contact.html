FAQ

Common questions

Quick answers to what we're most often asked by partners, municipalities, and investors.

What types of organisations do you partner with?
We work with city municipalities, development donors, UN agencies, impact investors, research institutions, NGOs, and cooperatives. Any organisation aligned with sustainable sanitation, MHM access, gender equity, or circular economy in Nepal is a potential partner.
How long does a HUB deployment take?
From site selection to operational handover, a standard Aerosan HUB deployment takes 4–7 months including site assessment, civil works, IoT installation, bio-digester commissioning, and full operator training.
What MHM facilities are included in every HUB?
Every HUB includes sanitary product vending machines, hygienic disposal bins, privacy screens, a clean changing space, and a dedicated female section with secure entry and enhanced safety lighting — MHM-compliant as a standard feature.
Does Aerosan require government funding to operate?
No. Each HUB generates operational surplus from five revenue streams — user fees, biogas sales, organic fertilizer, on-site advertising, and training licences. Government support may fund construction but is not required for operations.
Which branch should I contact for my city?
Use the branch filter above to find the office serving your province. For Bagmati Province contact Kathmandu HQ, for Gandaki contact Pokhara, for Koshi contact Biratnagar, and for Narayani contact Bharatpur. For other provinces, reach out to hello@aerosan.np.
How can researchers or journalists engage?
We welcome research collaborations, site visits, and media inquiries. Select "Research Partnership" or "Media & Press" in the form above and our team will respond within 48 hours to arrange an introductory call.
